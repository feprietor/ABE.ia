Ethical Design

Built with responsibility.

Farm decisions carry real financial weight. ABE is designed from the ground up to be trustworthy, fair, and protective of the farmers who use it.

🔒

Data Privacy First

ABE never sells farmer data to third parties. Profile information used for program screening is stored only to improve the relevance of your alerts — and only with your knowledge. Iowa farmers already distrust data-hungry platforms; ABE is designed to earn trust, not exploit it.

⚖️

Financial Fairness

Every financial output is calculated by deterministic Python code, not the AI. This is a non-negotiable design choice — research shows language models fail financial questions 81% of the time. No beginning farmer should make a six-figure leasing decision based on a hallucinated number.

📖

Radical Transparency

Every recommendation ABE makes includes citations to the source documents it used. If confidence falls below a threshold, ABE says so explicitly. Farmers can verify every answer themselves — ABE is a guide, not an oracle.

🌍

Inclusive by Design

ABE runs on Telegram — a free app available on any smartphone. No subscription, no rural broadband requirement, no technical knowledge needed. The system is also designed for offline resilience, so farmers in low-signal areas aren't excluded from the guidance they need.

🤝

Not a Replacement for Humans

ABE is not designed to replace ISU Extension agents, farm consultants, or lenders — it's designed to make their relationships with farmers more productive. When a situation requires human judgment, ABE says so and helps farmers find the right person to talk to.

🔄

Continuously Updatable

Because ABE retrieves from a curated document library rather than a trained model, knowledge stays current. When ISU updates its cost-of-production tables or the IFA changes program criteria, the knowledge base updates — no retraining, no risk of outdated answers baked into model weights.

⚠️

Important disclaimer: ABE provides decision-support information, not professional legal, financial, or agronomic advice. Always verify program eligibility with the Iowa Finance Authority or USDA directly, and consult a licensed professional for complex decisions. ABE's role is to inform your judgment — not to replace it.

Questions & Answers

Frequently Asked

Everything you need to know before your first conversation with ABE.

Is ABE free to use? ▾
Yes. ABE is designed to be free for Iowa beginning farmers. The mission is to eliminate the financial barrier to expert farm business guidance — not to add another subscription cost.
How do I access ABE? ▾
ABE runs as a Telegram bot. You don't need to install any special app — just open Telegram on your phone, find the ABE bot, and start a conversation. No setup, no dashboard to learn.
Can I trust ABE's financial numbers? ▾
All calculations — margins, rental benchmarks, cost estimates — are run by deterministic Python code, not the AI. Research shows LLMs fail financial questions 81% of the time when calculating directly; ABE sidesteps this entirely. Every number has a source you can verify.
What data does ABE use? ▾
ABE draws from ISU Extension's Ag Decision Maker series (crop costs, rental rates), USDA NASS and AMS for live Iowa commodity prices, Iowa Finance Authority program guides, and USDA FSA/EQIP documentation. All sources are publicly verifiable.
Does ABE store my data? ▾
ABE is designed with data privacy in mind. Farmer profile data used for program eligibility screening is stored only to improve the relevance of your alerts. ABE never shares your information with third parties.
What if ABE doesn't know the answer? ▾
If confidence falls below the threshold, ABE says so explicitly rather than guessing. It will direct you to the most relevant source and, where appropriate, suggest connecting with an ISU Extension agent or Iowa Finance Authority for complex, case-specific situations.
Does ABE work if I'm in a field with poor signal? ▾
ABE is being designed with offline resilience in mind. Core knowledge resources can be cached locally so ABE can answer most questions without a live connection, syncing when connectivity returns.
Is ABE only for corn and soybean farmers? ▾
The Phase 1 prototype focuses on Iowa corn and soybean operations — the most common beginning farmer profile. The skill-based architecture means additional crops, livestock, and specialty operations can be added as ABE expands beyond the competition period.
